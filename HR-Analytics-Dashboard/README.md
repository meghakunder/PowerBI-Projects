# HR Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview

- Analyzed employee workforce data using Power BI.
- Identified workforce trends and employee performance metrics.
- Tracked retrenchment insights and promotion eligibility.
- Analyzed overtime patterns and employee satisfaction.
- Created multi-page interactive HR analytics dashboard.
- Built summary, action, and detailed insight pages for HR decision-making.

---

## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Home Page
Provides an overall summary of the organization including:
- Total Employees
- Gender Distribution
- Employees on Service
- Retrenched Employees
- Promotion Eligibility
- Service Years Distribution
- Job Levels Distribution
- Distance Status Analysis

### 2. Action Page
Focuses on employees who:
- Are due for promotion
- May face retrenchment
- Require HR attention

This page was specifically designed to identify employees who are eligible for severance pay. 

The organization’s business requirement was to track employees who were due for promotion but were also marked for retrenchment, helping HR identify employees requiring compensation and severance-related actions.

### 3. Detail Page
Provides detailed analysis on:
- Job Satisfaction
- Overtime Analysis
- Department-wise Retrenchment
- Promotion Trends
- Job Role Analysis
- Employee Performance Metrics

## Key Insights

1. 92% of employees are currently active in service while only 8% are marked for retrenchment.

2. Research & Development department has the highest number of employees marked for retrenchment.

3. Entry and mid-level job positions (Level 1 and Level 2) contain the majority of the workforce.

4. Around 85% of employees are categorized as high-rated employees.

5. A significant percentage of employees are working overtime, which may impact employee satisfaction and retention.

6. Employees with approximately 5 years of service represent the highest employee count.

7. Only a small percentage of employees are currently due for promotion compared to the overall workforce.

8. Sales and Research & Development departments contain the majority of employees in the organization.

9. Manager and Sales Executive roles show comparatively higher retrenchment counts.

10. Most employees fall under the “Very Close” distance category, indicating reduced commuting distance for a large portion of staff.
